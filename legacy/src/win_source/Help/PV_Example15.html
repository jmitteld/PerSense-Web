Present value of past losses, with statutory rates by year
Your client had been earning $35,000 per year before a disabling accident in June, 1990. She had been receiving 4% wage increases each June. The case comes up for trial in 1994. Statute requires that interest on the losses be computed without compounding, using the average T-Bill rate in force for each year since 1990. What is the amount of the loss with interest.
Enter the $35,000 as biweekly payments of $1346.15. Enter 4% ANNual COLA. Enter the appropriate T-Bill rates at bottom, and specify SIMPLE interest. Press letter C and read the total present value of past wages in the cell at the lower right.
