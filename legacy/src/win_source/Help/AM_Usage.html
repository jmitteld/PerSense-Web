Using the Amortization Screen
Most often, you'll use the Amortization screen to compute the payment amount when the other loan parameters are known. But like all PerSense screens, the Amortization screen lets you enter any logical combination of data and fills in the blanks from there. Here are some examples
  • PerSense computes the payment amount if all the cells to the left of it are completed.
  • Specify the payment and PerSense calculates the loan rate.
  • Enter the payment and the loan rate. PerSense displays the duration of the loan.
  • If you specify all three - the payment amount, the loan rate and the number of periods - PerSense can print an amortization table based on the information you provided. However, if any of your numbers is not precisely correct, the principal balance won't reduce to zero on the specified date. If the loan is not fully repaid by the last payment date you have specified, PerSense will add a final balloon payment to the amortization schedule. Conversely, if the loan is fully repaid before the last payment date, PerSense will shorten the schedule and adjust the final payment.
  • PerSense calculates the loan rate if you specify the loan amount and the complete payment schedule.
  • PerSense displays the duration (number of periods and last payment date) if you enter the loan amount, rate, and payment amount(s).
  • The cell labeled Points is optional. If this cell is filled in, (even with zero), then an APR computation is performed for the loan, and the standard APR is displayed in the cell at right. (The APR cell is for output only.) Leave the Points cell blank if you don't need an APR.
