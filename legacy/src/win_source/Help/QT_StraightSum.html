Striaght sum of payments
You can check that the payments actually sum to 1,000,000 using a little computational trick. The present value with a zero interest rate is the same as the simple sum of the payments. You can ask Per%Sense to compute this for you on a second Present Value line. With the cursor on the first line in the bottom block, press Ctrl+C to copy that line. Then change the 7% rate to 0%. Press Enter once more, and the alternative value (really just the sum of the payments) appears at right.

Like all Per%Sense screens, the Present Value screen can work with any combination of data that you enter, as long as it logically determines an outcome. In the above example, we entered a set of payments and computed its present value. Alternatively, we could have specified the present value and left the rate blank to be computed by PerSense. This is, in fact, how to compute an internal rate of return (IRR). You can even fill in all three parts of the summary block (date, any rate or yield, and value) and leave blank an amount or date in either of the above two blocks. This strategy is useful if you are trying to devise an annuity or structured settlement with a targeted present value.
