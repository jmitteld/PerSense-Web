Example 13: Principal moratorium
On December 15, 1994, you borrow $150,000 to pay for a franchise store, and agree to repay the loan over 10 years at 10.5 percent. Because you expect revenues to be low during the start-up phase, you negotiate a principal moratorium for the first year. What is the payment amount from the second year forward?
A principal moratorium or Interest-only loan is a repayment structure in which monthly payments are just enough to cover interest, and principal remains constant during the first phase of the loan. Real repayment begins at a specified time thereafter.

Enter the amount borrowed, loan date, loan rate, first payment date, number of periods (120), and payments per year. Press C at this point to see what the monthly payment would be without a moratorium ($2,024.02).

Press PgDn three times, and Tab over to the Principal Repayment Delayed cell. Enter the date (one year from the first payment) on which you agree to begin the larger payment amount. Press C to compute that figure ($2,152.63). Press Ctrl-T to generate a table.
