Example 7: Double what if
How does the monthly payment on a $100,000 mortgage depend on the interest rate, from 7% to 9%, and on loan duration, from 15 to 30 years.
With the same commands for a What-if table, you can generate lines for all the different combinatorial possibilities associated with varying two or three different lines. Here we want to look at 9 different values of the interest rate, from 7% to 8.5%, and 4 different values ofthe loan term: 15, 20, 25, and 30 years. From the same screen as in Example 6 above, press Ctrl-T and when prompted for columns to vary, respond 6,7 Enter , indicating that you want to vary both columns 6 and 7. In the next line, the program now expects you to enter two numbers for increments, separated by a comma. The first number applies to column 6, and the second to column 7. Type -5, .25 Enter . (The increment for the number of years is negative because you're starting at 30 and incrementing downward to 25, 20, and 15.) Similarly, for the number of lines to write, there are 3 additional years lines and 6 additional rate lines. Type 3,6 Enter . 1116a225b WHAT-IF LINE VARYING ONE OR MORE ENTRIES Column(s) to vary ( for none): 6, 7 Increment: -5, .25 Number of lines to write: 3, 6 The total number of lines is now (3+1) x (6+1) = 28.
