Example 14: Targeted principal reduction
You borrow $150,000 for 10 years at 8.5 percent on February 1, 1995. You've agreed to reduce the principal by at least $1000 per month. What is your payment schedule?
In a normal amortization, the initial payments go mostly to interest, and very little principal is repaid. Today's wary lenders will sometimes negotiate an increase in loan payments in this initial phase, guaranteeing that the principal balance declines by a certain amount each month. This is known as Fixed payment to principal or Targeted Principal Reduction. If you put a number in this middle box at the bottom of the Extended Options screen, PerSense will increase the early payments as necessary to assure that at least this much principal is repaid each month.

Enter the basic loan information on the top line (as illustrated).

With Extended Options on, press PgDn three times, then Tab over to the Targeted Principal Reduction box. Enter 1000 and press C .

Notice that the payments are different each month at the beginning, but the portion of each payment going to principal is always $1000. After two years, the principal balance is lowered to the point where the regular payments guarantee more than a $1000 reduction in principal. At this point, regular payments take over and the payment amount remains constant for the remainder of the loan term.
