Amortization Screen
The Amortization screen is a flexible loan analysis system. You can use it to compute payments for a complex, structured loan, or to generate amortization tables for any kind of loan. In the screen that you see before you, just the basic options are active:

In this quick tour, we won't try any of the Extended Options, but you can peek at them by pressing X now:

Calculations are faster on the simple Amortization screen, but you'll need the Extended Options if your loan is structured with any of the following:
  • Balloon payments
  • Skipped payments
  • Variable interest rate
  • Fixed payment to principal (targeted principal reduction)
  • Delayed principal payment (principal moratorium)
Press X once more to turn the Extended Options off.

With the cursor in the cell labeled Amount borrowed, press Ctrl+V (for paste) to enter the Amount financed number from the Mortgage screen.

Then enter the settlement or closing date for your mortgage (labeled on the screen as Loan Date), as well as the rate quoted by the bank (Loan Rate %). The first payment date is generally one month after the Loan Date; if it's more or less, Per%Sense's Computational Settings screen offers options for treating the partial month's interest.

Enter the Number of Periods (12 times the number of years) and the Payments per year (12 for monthly payments).

If you press Enter now, Per%Sense will calculate your monthly payment, and the amount should be the same as the number computed on the Mortgage screen, less the Monthly Tax+Ins figure, which isn't part of the amortization.
