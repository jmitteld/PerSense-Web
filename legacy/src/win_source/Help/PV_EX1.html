Example 1: Present value of a simple annuity
What is the value of an annuity that pays 1000 per month for the next 20 years?
To answer this question, we enter the Periodic Payments in the upper ight of the screen, then summarize them in the bottom block labeled Present Value.

Note: The annuity in this example has a fixed time interval. To calculate an annuity that continues "for life", use the Actuarial window. (See Example 23, page 152.)

The From date should be today's date (or whenever payments begin), and the Through date should be twenty years later, less one month.

Press PgDn twice to reach the bottom block. Enter today's date (press letter T ) at the bottom, and choose an appropriate interest rate.

Press C to compute the answer, which you may read from the Value cell at the lower right.
