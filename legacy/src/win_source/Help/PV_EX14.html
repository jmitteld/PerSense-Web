Example 14: Value of a discounted loan: original interest rate known
Suppose a loan was taken out 12/1/86 for $100,000 at 13%, amortized over 20 years with monthly payments. How much should you pay for this loan in June of 1995 in order to realize a 9.6% rate of return?
How much should you pay for a discounted loan in order to realize a specific rate of return? This is essentially the same computation as the Example above, except that you must first compute the payment amount.

Enter the loan date (12/1/86), loan rate (13), and amount (100,000) in the bottom block.

List the From Date (1/1/87), the Through date (12/1/06), and 12 times Per Year in the Periodic Payments block. Leave the Amount column blank. Press C to compute the monthly payment.

You also must remember to change the From date to the next payment after the present. (You will not be receiving the earlier payments, so they do not count toward your present value.)

Now change the date in the Present Value block to the date you plan to purchase the loan. The amount should be left unknown, but specify a True Rate of 9.6%. Press C and read the amount from the bottom right cell. You should offer $97,867.60 to repurchase the loan.
