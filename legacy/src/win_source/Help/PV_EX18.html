Example 18: Paying a year's rent in advance
Your building's landlord offers a 10% discount for paying a year's rent on January 1. Should you accept the offer?
This example is included not because there is any particular trick involved in the computation, but because the results are surprising.

To put the question another way, if you choose to prepay the rent, what is the effective rate of return on your money?

Let's say your rent is $5,000 per month. Compute the amount you would have to pay on January 1 with the pop-up calculator: Press Alt-K , then 5000 * 12 10% =. The answer is $54,000. Place this amount in the Present Value block, As of the first of January.

Leave the rate to be computed. Enter the alternative monthly payments in the Periodic Payments block above. Press C to compute the return.

The true rate that appears is more than 23%. This is a good investment, indeed! There are two reasons why the figure is so high. First, a 10% discount corresponds to an 11.11% rate of return. This is because the return is computed on the portion invested (90%), not the return (100%), and 10 90 = 11.11%. Second, you are not actually paying a full year in advance. The monthly payments would have been spread out from 0 to 11 months from January 1, so you are getting your 11.11% over roughly 5 months.
