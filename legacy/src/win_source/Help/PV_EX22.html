Example 22: Present value of past losses with statutory rates by year
You represent a client who had been earning $35,000 per year before a disabling accident in June, 1990. She had been receiving 4% wage increases each June. The case comes up for trial in 1994. Statute requires that interest on the losses be computed without compounding, using the average T-Bill rate in force for each ar since 1990. What is the amount of the loss with interest?

Enter the $35,000 as biweekly payments of $1346.15, with a 4% COLA. Enter the appropriate T-Bill rates at bottom, and specify SIMPLE interest. Press letter C and read the total present value of past wages in the cell at the lower right ($155,226.01).

Note: The columns labeled True Rate, Loan Rate and Yield usually hold different numbers. A loan rate is a true rate compounded over one interest period; and a yield is a true rate compounded over one year. If you specify SIMPLE interest, there is no compounding at all, and the distinction among these three terms vanishes. Hence the three columns hold the same number, and all three have the screen attributes of input numbers.
