Example 9: Calculating the completion date
A loan for $85,000 is taken out on January 18, 1995 at 7 percent interest. Repayment is to be in $1,000 monthly installments for the first three years, $1,500 per month thereafter until the loan is paid off. What is that date?
Enter the loan information on the first line, using the starting payment of $1000. Leave the Number of Periods blank - it is the target of this computation.

Press PgDn twice to move to the Changes block. The date you use for the change must be before the new payment amount starts. Here we've used 2/1/98, even though the first payment at this amount won't begin until 3/1/98. Enter the same rate (7%), and the new payment amount, $1500.

Press C to compute the number of periods (and last payment date).

When you print a table with Ctrl-T , the payments start at $1,000 and change to $1,500 on the next payment after the specified date. The final payment is adjusted to bring the balance exactly to zero.
