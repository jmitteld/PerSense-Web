Example 4: Terminating a 30-year mortgage with a 15-year balloon
An 8.1 percent mortgage for $240,000 is amortized at 30 years but capped with a 15-year balloon. What is the amount of the payments and the balloon?
Suppose you plan to live in a house for 5 to 10 years, and you want to minimize your monthly mortgage payments. A 30-year mortgage offers lower monthly payments than a 15-year mortgage. But often you can do even better than this: The rate for a 30-year mortgage is higher than the 15-year rate, because the bank must assume the risk that economic conditions will change in the coming decades. Wouldn't it be best of all to get the low, 30-year payments with the 15-year interest rate? Some banks will do this, with the condition that you terminate the mortgage after 15 years. In other words, after 15 years, you must either pay off the remainder of the loan or refinance at then-current rates.

  • Do this computation in two steps.
