Example 16: Composite interest of a bond issue with multiple bonds
A company issues three bond series simultaneously: $20 million with 9% coupon, maturing in 7 years; $30 million with 9-1/8% coupon maturing in 10 years; and $30 million with 9-1/4% coupon maturing in 15 years. If all are sold at par, what is the cost of borrowing $80 million?
Note: This example is entered in thousands rather than millions to save space on e screen. This will not affect the interest rate computation.

This simple extension of Example 15 lists three entries, one for each bond. Because the evaluation is done on the issue date (which is an anniversary date), no accrued interest exists to complicate the problem.

The bond maturities are lump sum repayments placed in the Single Payments column. The amount is simply the issue value of the bond.

The coupons (or dividends) are paid semi-annually, and are listed as Periodic Payments. To compute the coupon amounts, use the pop-up calculator ( Alt-K ) to multiply each bond value by the stated coupon amount, and divide by 2 for the semi-annual payments).

20,000 x .09 2 = 900.00
30,000 x .09125 2 = 1,368.75
30,000 x .0925 2 = 1,387.50

Enter the issue date (8/1/94) and the price received for the bonds ($80,000 total) in the Present Value block, leaving the rate to be computed. In practice, if commissions, costs and insurance were taken out of the proceeds of the bond issue, the amount should be the net proceeds from the sale.
