Example 4: Accelerating a loan with more frequent payments
You're paying off a 30-year, $100,000, 10-percent mortgage with monthly payments. If you could pay half your monthly mortgage payment every two weeks, how much more quickly would your mortgage be paid off?
The loan will be paid off faster because two weeks is less than half a month. "Every other week" is 26 half payments, about equivalent to 13 full payments per year instead of 12.

This problem is computed in two parts. First, we'll compute the payment amount of the original loan. Then we'll change the frequency and compute the duration of the modified schedule.

Enter the Amount, Loan Date, Rate, 1st Payment Date, 360 Periods, and 12 Payments Per Year. Press C to compute the monthly payment.

Divide the payment by two and enter the result, Use the pop-up calculator to divide the payment amount by two: 438.78.

Now change the frequency of payments:
  • In the Payments per Year column, change 12 (monthly) to 26 (bi-weekly).
  • Erase the Number of Periods entry - this is what you want PerSense to compute.
  • Press C to compute the term of the loan. (Note that the setting for 360-day computations is changed to 365 for you automatically - weekly and biweekly loans are never computed on a 360-day calendar.)

The answer (542) is the number of two-week periods in your new mortgage. The final payment is scheduled for 2015 - a full 10 years earlier than the original.

The striking thing about this example is that a difference in payments that most families would not even notice can shave nine years off a 30-year, 10 percent mortgage. This is because in the first years of a 30-year mortgage, nearly all the payment goes to interest, and only about $44 out of the $877 monthly payment goes to principal initially. If the monthly payment is effectively increased (by making payments more frequently), the difference goes entirely into principal repayment.
