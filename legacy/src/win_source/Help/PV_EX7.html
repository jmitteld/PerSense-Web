Example 7: Future value of aperiodic deposit
At the beginning of 1995, you create a pension fund for your employees. You opened the account with a $30,000 deposit and plan to make quarterly deposits of $10,000 for the next five years in an account paying 7% interest. How much will the fund be worth at the end of that time?
In the Single Payments block, enter 1/1/95 with a $30,000 amount.

Press PgDn to reach the Periodic Payments block. Use the date of the first deposit as the From date (the example uses the date three months from January 1, or 4/1/95) and the date of the last deposit as the Through date. The example uses the date of the twentieth payment, 1/1/00.

Enter 4 Per Yr and the amount (10,000). Press PgDn to reach the bottom block.

Enter the date five years from now (1/1/00), and 7 in the True Rate column.

Press Enter or C to complete the computation.
