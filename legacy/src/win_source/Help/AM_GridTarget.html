Target Grid
In this cell, specify a minimum amount by which the principal balance should decrease each month. If the specified payment is not sufficient to cover interest plus the targeted principal reduction, the payment amount in the table will be temporarily augmented to that level.

In loans that include Targeted Principal Reduction, the first payment is the highest, and payments thereafter decrease steadily because they cover a lower interest cost plus the same principal reduction target. At some point, the principal balance is sufficiently low that principal reduction is naturally greater than the targeted amount, and the payments thereafter will be constant. It is this constant amount that is reflected in the Payment column of the top line.

The term interest plus principal is used to describe some loans in which the initial principal is divided into N equal portions, and monthly payments each consist of this constant principal portion plus a variable interest calculated for that month. This is a special case of Targeted Principal Reduction in which the "normal" monthly payment doesn't ever come into play. To compute interest plus principal loans, simply fill in zero for the payment amount on the top line. For convenience, PerSense provides a shortcut to dividing the principal by the number of payments: with the cursor in the Targeted Principal Reduction cell, press letter F (for fixed) to compute the payment amount for this method.
