Example 9: Structuring a settlement to cover the attorneys' fee
In Example 8 above, you wish to restructure the settlement so that the plaintiff is able to pay your fee up front, computed at 1/3 of the settlement's net present value.
Notice that the $50,000 received up front is far too small to cover a fee calculated as 1/3 of the present value of the settlement (1/3 x $441,856.43 = 147,285.48). Without changing the total value of the settlement, how could you restructure it so that your fee can be paid up front?

If the up-front amount is to be increased, either the monthly payments or the terminal payment must be reduced. Looking at the value of the terminal payment ($10,228.42) it is clear that there is not enough value there to contribute substantially to your goal of $147,285.48. So the obvious place to make the reduction is in the monthly amount.

First harden the $441,856.43 total value figure in the lower right; this was originally a calculated number, but now you want to hold it constant. To harden the figure, move the cursor to that cell and press letter H . It now has the attributes of an input number.

Erase the $2,000 monthly amount, then replace the $50,000 single payment on today's date with the amount ($147,285.48) that you need for your fee. Press letter C to recalculate.
