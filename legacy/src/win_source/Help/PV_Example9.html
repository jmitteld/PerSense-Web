How is APR actually computed?
This example illustrates how an APR is actually computed - though in fact the computation is automatic on the Amortization and Mortgage Screens.
A loan of $100,000 is taken out on 1/1/92, with repayment over 10 years to begin 2/1/92. The loan rate is 12.0%, and the APR would be just 12%, but for 2.5 points paid at settlement. What APR should be quoted?

First compute the monthly payment: Enter loan date and 12% Loan Rate in bottom block. Enter the payment dates above, but leave the amount blank. Press C to compute Periodic Payment amount, above.
The true amount received for the loan is 97,500 (= 100,000 - 2.5%). A "Loan Rate" computed with this loan amount but with the payment amount corresponding to a 100,000 loan is the desired APR (12.63%).
