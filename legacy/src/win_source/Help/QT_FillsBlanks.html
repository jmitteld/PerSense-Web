Per%Sense fills in the blanks
All three screens are organized as column-and-row spreadsheets with pre-programmed formulas. What is special about Per%Sense is that the inverse of every formula is also programmed, along with a smart algorithm that decides what computation is necessary. This means that you can leave any cell on any screen blank; when enough information has been entered on the rest of the screen, Per%Sense will compute that cell the next time you press Enter or C. For example, you can compute loan payments in the Amortization screen by filling in loan amount, dates, and the interest rate. But on the same screen, you can also leave the interest rate blank, and fill in the monthly payment instead. This time it is the rate that is computed. Or you can leave fill in both the rate and the payment, but leave the number of payments blank, for Per%Sense to compute. Or, filling in all of these, you can ask Per%Sense to compute the original loan amount, simply by leaving that cell blank. This complete flexibility is the feature that gives Per%Sense its power.

Numbers that you enter yourself (input) appear in normal font on the screen. Numbers that Per%Sense computes for you (output) appear in bold font. Every time you press Enter or C , the output numbers are recomputed, but the input numbers are preserved.

Move the cursor about the screen with the four arrow keys and the Tab key. Shift+Tab moves the cursor leftward one column. Some screens are organized in blocks, outlined in double lines. PgUp and PgDn move the cursor to the next block and the previous block, respectively.
