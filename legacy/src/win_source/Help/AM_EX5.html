Example 5: Reverse mortgage
You are 66 years old and recently retired. Your house is fully paid for and unencumbered. You take out a reverse mortgage to help with living expenses with $2000 per month. What will the total debt be when you are 80?
Reverse mortgages are becoming a favored instrument for providing income to retired people whose principal asset is a home. Nothing is borrowed up-front, but the borrower receives monthly payments from the bank, usually with a home as collateral. Interest and principal both grow rapidly with time.

To model a reverse mortgage, simply set the Amount borrowed equal to zero and fill in the monthly Payment from the bank to the borrower as a negative value. Press Ctrl-T to see how the loan balance progresses with time, or fill in a date in the lower right to obtain a Balance as of any given date.
