Negative amortization
In Nagative Amortization loans, the payment amount is computed as if a lowe interest rate were in effect; but interest is really accruing at a higher rate. After a set time, the payment amount increases to reflect not only the real interest rate, but also the extra principal which has been accumulating, since the principal part of each month's pmt has been negative. To access the Advanced Amortization screen, press Alt+x.
Enter the amount and dates of the loan. Fill in the low rate (7%). Press <Enter> to compute the payment amount. Move the cursor to the payment cell and press H to "harden" the entry. Now that you've computed the payment, you can chance the loan rate from 7% to a higher rate (11%). Enter a rate "change" to 11% after 5 years. Although the rate hasn't changed, this will cause the payment amount to be recomputed at that time.
