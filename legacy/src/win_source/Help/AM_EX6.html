Example 6: Adjustable rate loan
At the beginning of 1988, a 20-year adjustable rate loan was issued for $277,400 at 11%. On June 1, the rate declined half a percent, and the payments were adjusted accordingly. Each six months after that, the rate continued to drop half a percent, until reaching a plateau of 7% in 1996. Print an amortization table , assuming the rate stays the same for the remaining life of the loan?
This is the most common use of the block labeled Rate Changes and Changes in Payment (the Changes block, for short): Enter the rate changes at their points in time, and let PerSense re-amortize the loan and compute new payment amounts.

Enter the starting terms of the loan on the first line of the screen. Monthly payments are scheduled throughout the loan.

Press PgDn twice to reach the Changes block.
Enter the eight changes in rates with their effective dates.
Press Enter to compute the different payment amounts.
Press Ctrl-T to obtained a detailed amortization table for the loan.
