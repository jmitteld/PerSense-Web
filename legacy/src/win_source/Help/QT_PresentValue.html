Present Value Screen
The Present Value screen is a convenient tool for computing structured legal settlements, annuities, pensions, and internal rates of return. If you are adept - or become adept - with the concept of present value, you will find it a very powerful paradigm for analyzing many other sorts of financial questions as well. Loans can be modeled on the Present Value screen as well, but this screen is rarely preferable to the Amortization screen for this purpose.

The top part of the screen is a list of payments: lump sum payments on the left, and periodic payments on the right. There's space for up to 127 lump sums and 127 sets of periodic payments, each with its own start and stop date. The bottom block of the screen summarizes the top two blocks. The number at the far right is the present value of all payments listed above and is equal to the sum of the "partial present values" listed to the right of each entry in the upper screen.

You have room for three such summaries, perhaps using different interest rates or As of dates. To model a loan, use the bottom block for the loan date, rate, and amount; use the top two blocks for payments.
