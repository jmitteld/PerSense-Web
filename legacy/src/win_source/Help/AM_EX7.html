Example 7: Including balloon payments
A $333,000 loan closes in December 1994. The term is 10 years, and 9.9% percent interest is charged. In addition to regular monthly payments, balloon payments of $80,000 are scheduled at the beginning of 1998, 2000 and 2002. What is the amount of the monthly payment?
Note: Go to your Computational Settings window (press Alt-S ) and change the line labeled Stated balloon includes regular pmt to NO. In this example, balloon payments are in addition to normal monthly payments.

Enter the starting terms of the loan on the first line. If you press C now, you'll see a payment of $4,382.20. This is what the regular monthly payment would be if no balloon payments had been built into the loan.

With Extended Options on, press PgDn twice to reach the Balloon Payments block.

Enter the three balloons and their dates. (Press Shift - Enter to move to the beginning of the next line in the same block.)

When you press C again, the payment on the first line drops to $2,504.64.
