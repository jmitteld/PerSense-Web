Example 19: The 90% test for a capital lease with skipped payments
You have just signed a seven-year lease agreement for machine tools valued at $90,000. The terms of the lease are: $2,000 at the inception of the lease and $1,000 per month for the remainder of the lease, except no payments in December. It is estimated that the tools will be worth $35,000 at the end of the ase. The last time you borrowed from a bank you paid 6.8% interest. Apply the 90% test to this lease.
Some leases are treated as capital expenditures for accounting purposes, and others as operating expenditures. One of the FASB criteria for a capital lease is that the present value of all lease payments plus the fair market value of the leased item at the end of the lease be at least 90% of the fair market value at the beginning of the lease. The interest rate to be used in this computation is the lessor's incremental borrowing rate, that is, the rate the firm would have to pay a bank to finance a purchase of the same equipment.

Put the value of the tools at the end of the lease and the $2,000 payment in the Single Payments block.

The $1,000 payments go in the Periodic Payments column. Add a negative $1,000 payment every December 1st to indicate a skipped payment. At the bottom of the screen, enter the date of the lease and the 6.8% bank rate. Compute the present value by pressing C .
