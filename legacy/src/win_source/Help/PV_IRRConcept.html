IRR: the Concept
IRR stands for Internal Rate of Return. This is the number you want to know when you ask, "How well did my investment do last year?". It is the true measure of investment performance over time. Sometimes people speak of a time-weighted average return; properly computed, this is no different from an IRR.

IRR is also very closely related to such concepts as bond yield and APR; examples in this chapter will show you how to compute these quantities on the Present Value screen.

Why doesn't Per%Sense have an IRR screen? Why are IRR calculations done on the Present Value screen? Because the calculations are really one and the same. If you ask "What is the interest rate that makes the present value of these payments equal to $100,000?", you are asking precisely for an IRR. Conversely, if you ask, "How much should I bid for this investment instrument if I want to achieve a 10% IRR?", you are asking for a present value.
