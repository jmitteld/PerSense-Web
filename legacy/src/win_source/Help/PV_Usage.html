Using the Present Value Screen
The Variable Rate Present Value screen computes present values where the interest rate is a function of time. This is just what is needed for computing interest on past due taxes, and for valuation of legal claims for past damages using variable interest rates. Press V to reach this screen.

The top part of the screen is a list of payments: lump sum payments on the left and periodic payments on the right. The screen accommodates up to 10 lump sums and 10 sets of periodic payments, each with its own start and stop date.

The bottom block of the screen summarizes the top two blocks. The total Value at the bottom right is the present value of all payments listed above, and is equal to the sum of the Values (actually, "partial present values") listed to the right of each entry in the upper screen. There is room for three such summaries, perhaps using different interest rates or As of different dates. To model a loan, use the bottom block for the loan date, rate, and amount; use the top two blocks for payments. (It is possible to calculate loans here in the Present Value screen, but it is rarely preferable to the Amortization screen for this purpose.)

For IRR computations, the date and amount of the original investment should be entered in the bottom block, and all money that comes back to you is listed in the upper part of the screen. Additions to your investment may be included as negative entries in the upper screen.
