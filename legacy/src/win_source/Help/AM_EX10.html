Example 10: Negative amortization: Payment amount specified
An eight-year loan is taken out on March 1, 1994 for $500,000 at 8.8 percent. Monthly payments will be $5,000 during years one and two, $7,000 during years three and four, and after that, just enough to complete repayment in four additional years. What is the payment amount during the last four years?
Note: The term negative amortization describes any loan structure in which the regular payments are insufficient to pay the interest that's accruing, let alone reduce the principal. Of course, the piper must be paid at a later date when payments increase, or a large balloon payment is mandated.

Enter the amount, date, and starting terms of the loan on the first line of the Amortization screen.

In the Extended Options block for Changes, enter the Date of the last $5,000 payment, the same 8.8% rate, and an Amount of $7,000. (Remember that, with interest in arrears, the rate doesn't show up in the amortization schedule until the month after the date entered for the change.)

In the next line, enter the Date of the last $7,000 payment, and the Rate, but leave the Amount blank.

PerSense will compute a payment that makes the loan complete on the specified date.
