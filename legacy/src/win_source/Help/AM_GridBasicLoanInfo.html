Basic Loan Information Grid
This is where you enter basic information about the loan:
  • Amount Borrowed - The amount of money you are borrowing.
  • Loan Date - The day that you borrow the money.
  • Loan Rate - The rate of the loan.
  • First Pmt Date - The date of your first payment.
  • # of Pds - The number of payments in this loan.
  • Pmts / Year - The number of payments in a year.
  • Payment - The amount of each payment.
  • Pts - Points are a one-time bank charge at the time a loan is taken out. 1.0 points means one percent of the amount borrowed is paid the the bank at settlement.
  • APR % - This read only column tells you the computed APR.
Enter amount borrowed and loan date, followed by rate and date of first payment. Enter the payments per year (e.g., 12 for monthly) and the total number of periods (e.g., 120 for 10 years of monthly payments).

Enter either interest rate or periodic payment amount - the other will be computed for you. You may enter both if you like, and leave the loan amount blank, to be computed. If you enter all three of these, you are in "manual override". Per%Sense, will proceed to make a table based on your numbers, but then seeing to it that repayment completes on the specified date is up to you.

Press for computation of either payment or rate, or press to generate a table. (You will be prompted for formatting details and other output options.)

Points and APR

The last two columns at the top right of the Basic Loan Information grid are an optional area for computing the APR of a loan. APR is a standard measure of true interest cost, including points charged up front. If you fill in the points (in %), the APR will be computed for you when you press letter or .
