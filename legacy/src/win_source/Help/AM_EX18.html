Example 18: Computing the loan rate when all payments are fixed
135,000 is borrowed in June, 1994. Repayment terms are: $1800/month for the first year, $2,200/month for the second year, and $2,700 for four more years thereafter. What is the interest rate?
Occasionally, the repayment terms of a loan will be agreed upon in dollar amounts, and you will want to back into the loan rate. For simple loans that fit on the first line of the screen, you can simply leave the loan rate blank to get your answer. This example is slightly more involved, and we'll demonstrate two ways to get the answer.

The first method is to use the Additional periodic pmts lines, just below the top line of the screen. Usually, these are used for payments that are contemporaneous with the payments on the top line, but on a different schedule -for example, yearly payments in addition to the regular monthly payments. But there is no reason that the three lines cannot be used for the first year, second year, and third year's payments, respectively. Simply leave the Loan Rate cell blank, and it is computed for you.

Set up the problem for 72 payments in all, and enter the two payment changes in the Changes block at the right of the screen, midway down. Leave the rates blank.

Because the Changes block is used, PerSense computes this loan as if it were a variable rate loan; three distinct interest rates are calculated. What is the equivalent fixed rate for this loan as a whole? This is an APR calculation, and can be done simply by putting a zero in the Points cell. (Remember that APR calculations tend to be slow, so they are only done when they are specifically requested. Putting any number, including a zero, in the Points cell.)
