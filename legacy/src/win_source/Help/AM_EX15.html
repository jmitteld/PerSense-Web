Example 15: Interest plus principal
In a $300,000, 6% business loan, quarterly payments are to be made covering interest plus 1/12 of total principal, so that the loan is paid off after 12 quarters. Print an amortization schedule.
This is a common specification for loan repayment, and it is easily accommodated in PerSense using the Targeted Principal Reduction specification. The trick is to make sure that the "regular" payment never appears.

Since the "regular" payment and the TPR are alternative minima for each payment, this can be accomplished by setting the regular payment to zero; then the TPR will always prevail.

To determine the TPR amount, just divide $300,000 by 12. For convenience, PerSense will do this for you if you press letter F in the TPR cell.
