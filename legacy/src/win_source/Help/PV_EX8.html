Exmaple 8: A structured legal settlement
You represent a plaintiff who has been offered a structured settlement for a disability claim. $50,000 is paid up front, plus an income of $2000 per month for thirty years, increasing at 3% per year, and a $100,000 lump sum at the end. hat is the amount of the settlement? What is its value?
The "amount" of the settlement, the simple sum of the payments, is a misleadingly large figure; nevertheless it is a number commonly cited by those who wish to make the settlement appear large. The present value of the payments, computed with a market interest rate, is a more meaningful number.

The way to compute a simple sum of payments is to enter a rate of zero in the Present Value block. Then the value is just the sum of the payments, independent of the As of date.

Enter the two lump sum payments with their dates in the upper left.

Enter $2000/month with the appropriate dates in the Periodic Payments block.

Move to the Present Value box and enter today's date and the assumed interest rate. (Here we've used 7.6 percent.)

Copy this line to the first line at the bottom with Ctrl-C and change the interest rate to zero to compute the sum of payments.

Press Enter or C to compute.

The total present value of all of the above payments ($441,856.43) appears in the Value cell of the Present Value block. $1,291,810.00 is the simple sum of the payments, unadjusted for time value. The partial present values ($50,000.00, $10,228.42, and $381,628.01) appear in their respective value cells on the upper portion of the screen.

Note: Observe the huge discrepancy between the sum of the payments ($1,291,810) and the present value ($441,856.43). This is because so much of the settlement is to be received 30 years in the future.
